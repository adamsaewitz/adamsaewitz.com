I’m fascinated with the craft of cinema (lots of love to Kubrick, Carpenter, De Sica, Herzog, Koreeada, Glazer) When I was a teenager I saw Apocalypse Now in a film class and something clicked for me. I don’t know how to express what I felt, something between Brando and Hopper and the stereophonic sound design with the Doors and when Coppola got Martin Sheen drunk for the bedroom scene and then the cows getting hacked. It just all left a powerful emotional impression on me. Like a sponge I embarked on a quest to learn every photoshop tutorial and iMovie demo I could grab ahold of and began thinking about the power of cinema and the language of it, how it is used, how it is shaped to communicate ideas (another favorite film back then was Run Lola Run!). Fast forward to the present and now I’m 27 and find myself still deeply affected by the visual medium and how it connects with human rights work as a documentary filmmaker. I see my passion for filmmaking now as a bridge to communicate my affection for those I photograph. If you’re curious to chat, please write to me as I’d love to hear from you.


A bit about me: In 2014, I photographed director Dream Hampton’s feature length documentary TREASURE – a film about the lives of transgender youth in Detroit, specifically focusing my lens on the story of Shelly Hilliard, a teenager who was brutally murdered. The film premiered in June, 2015 at the Los Angeles Film Festival and is currently touring film festivals nationwide.


My work has also been screened abroad in Ethiopia and at home at the Museum of Contemporary African Diasporan Arts in Brooklyn.

In addition to feature length and short form work, I have also edited branded content for Chanel, Electus, Equinox Gyms, Comcast, Kiehls, and Atlantic Records.
