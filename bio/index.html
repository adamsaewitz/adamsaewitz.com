When I was seventeen my high school art teacher screened Apocalypse Now. In the after glow of standing up and walking away from the computer, something ineffable bore itself inside of me. Between fat Brando and the advent of stereophonic sound design with the helicopters whirring and the Doors blasting under napalm or Dennis Hopper’s mind state or how Coppola built the montage of oxen getting hacked with machetes, my memory of the film and the experience of watching it is traced with admiration and awe in the craft and performance and has left an indelible mark on me. Like a sponge I spent the next year after school fixated in my bedroom toying with photoshop tutorials and cutting up iMovie stock footage. And when my father saw my curiosity bloom he bought me a camera and so I was quickly making images, too. Over the next couple years I was finding films I connected with – Almodovar and Kubrick, Carpenter and De Sica, Jean Renoir, Herzog, Koreeada, Kiarostami, Wiseman, Glazer. It’s such a cliché but it was like each filmmaker opened a path into a new world, a new lens, a new way of seeing. I barf a bit in my mouth reading that back but it is nonetheless true, I was in awe of these films and filmmakers and movies offered me a intimacy into the world in a way unlike other art did. I was hooked.


Fast forward to 2014, I worked as a cinemetagropher and photographed director Dream Hampton’s documentary TREASURE – a feature length film about the dangerous and vulnerable lives of transgender youth in Detroit, specifically focusing my lens on the story of Shelly Hilliard a teenager who was brutally murdered. The film premiered in June, 2015 at the Los Angeles Film Festival and is currently touring film festivals nationwide.


I freelance now in New York and work as a director, cinematographer and editor. My videos have screened abroad in Ethiopia and at home at the Museum of Contemporary African Diasporan Arts in Brooklyn. I’m currently interested in making documentaries and/or rap videos. I’m currently directing a short documentary about cystic fibrosis research and precision medicine and am interested in continuing to explore documentary filmmaking. In addition to feature length and short form work as a cinematographer, I have also edited branded content for the New York Times, Chanel, Electus, Equinox Gyms, Comcast, Kiehls, and Atlantic Records.
