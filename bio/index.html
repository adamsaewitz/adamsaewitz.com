When I was seventeen I saw Apocalypse Now and something ineffable bore itself inside of me. Between fat Brando and the advent of stereophonic sound design with the helicopters whirring and the Doors blasting under napalm or Dennis Hopper’s mind state or how Coppola built the montage of oxen getting hacked with machetes, my memory of the film and the experience of watching it is traced with admiration and awe in the craft and performance. The experience left an indelible mark on me. Like a sponge I spent the next year fixated in my bedroom after school toying with photoshop tutorials and cutting up iMovie stock footage until I slowly began taking images on my front lawn and re-discovering the power of photography in the world of digital. Over the next couple years I started finding films I connected with, too – Almodovar and Kubrick, Carpenter and De Sica, Jean Renoir, Herzog, Koreeada, Kiarostami, Wiseman, Glazer. It was like each filmmaker opened a path into a new world, a new lens, a new way of seeing. I was in awe of these films and filmmakers and the films offered a intimacy into a world very much akin to a novelist but the moving image fascinated me.


Fast forward to 2014, I worked as a cinemetagropher and photographed director Dream Hampton’s documentary TREASURE – a feature length film about the dangerous and vulnerable lives of transgender youth in Detroit, specifically focusing my lens on the story of Shelly Hilliard a teenager who was brutally murdered. The film premiered in June, 2015 at the Los Angeles Film Festival and is currently touring film festivals nationwide.


I work as a director and editor and my films have screened abroad in Ethiopia and at home at the Museum of Contemporary African Diasporan Arts in Brooklyn.


In addition to feature length and short form work as a cinematographer, I have also edited branded content for the New York Times, Chanel, Electus, Equinox Gyms, Comcast, Kiehls, and Atlantic Records.


I’m currently directing a short documentary about cystic fibrosis research and precision medicine and am interested in continuing to explore documentary filmmaking.
