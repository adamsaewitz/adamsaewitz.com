When I was seventeen I saw Apocalypse Now and it blew my mind. Between seeing a fat Brando and the advent of stereophonic sound design with the helicopters whirring and the Doors opening under napalm and Dennis Hopper’s performance and then the montage of oxen getting hacked with machetes, the confluence of craft and performance left an indelible mark on me. Like a sponge I spent the next year fixated on learning photoshop tutorials and playing with iMovie demos I slowly began playing with video and sunk my teeth into the langauge of the craft. I started finding films I connected with, too – Almodovar and Kubrick, Carpenter and De Sica, Jean Renoir, Herzog, Koreeada, Kiarostami, Wiseman, Glazer. It was like each filmmaker opened a path into a new world. I was in awe of these films and filmmakers.


In 2014, I photographed director Dream Hampton’s documentary TREASURE – a feature length film about the lives of transgender youth, specifically focusing my lens on the story of Shelly Hilliard, a teenager who was brutally murdered. The film was shot over a month in Detroit and premiered in June, 2015 at the Los Angeles Film Festival and is currently touring film festivals nationwide.


My work has also been screened abroad in Ethiopia and at home at the Museum of Contemporary African Diasporan Arts in Brooklyn.


In addition to feature length and short form work as a cinematographer, I have also edited branded content for Chanel, Electus, Equinox Gyms, Comcast, Kiehls, and Atlantic Records.


I’m currently directing a short documentary about cystic fibrosis research and its relationship within the emerging world of precision medicine and am interested in continuing to explore making documentaries that are rooted in the world of investigative journalism.
